About Us

To demystify molecular biology for youths in Kenya through education outreach and public engagement-making science accessible ,relevant and empowering for all Genes for change KE is a youth led initiative transforming the way Kenyan students and communities understand molecular biology and genetics .through science education, hands on workshop and public health awareness ,we connect the power of DNA to the issues that matter most from hereditary diseases to future careers in stem .we believe science isnt just for labs -its for life and it start here.

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To ignite curiosity and confidence in science among Kenyan children aged 7–17 by making genetics and molecular biology accessible, engaging, and culturally relevant — starting in the classroom, and reaching into communities.

Our Vision

A future where young African learners are not just science-literate, but science-leaders — pioneering research, innovating solutions, and transforming their communities through the power of STEM.

Our Story

Genes for Change started with a simple yet powerful idea — what if science didn’t wait until university? What if it began in the curiosity of a 10-year-old?

The Question

We asked: why don't more children in Kenya see themselves in science? Why are labs and DNA concepts reserved for later in life?

The Beginning

Founded by Kenyan educators and scientists, we launched a pilot program in 3 schools to explore hands-on DNA learning at the primary level.

Discovery

We saw it work — students became more engaged, confident, and excited about science. Some even asked for lab coats!

Momentum

With growing demand, we expanded across Kenya, training teachers, building partnerships, and developing Africa-specific genetics content.

The Future

We envision a continent where molecular biology is part of early learning, and where the next gene-editing pioneer might come from a village classroom.

Our Values

Scientific Curiosity

We foster questioning minds and a culture of wonder.

Equity in STEM

Every child deserves access to quality science education, regardless of gender, geography, or income.

Early Exposure

We believe science should start young, when minds are most open and eager to explore.

Local Relevance

We connect molecular biology to African challenges — from malaria genetics to food security.

Empowerment Through Knowledge

We demystify science, showing students that they belong in labs, classrooms, and beyond.

Why It Matters

Africa faces challenges that science can solve — from disease to climate change, from food shortages to innovation. But solving them starts with education.

Science Solves Real Problems

Understanding genetics empowers young people to fight disease, improve food systems, and lead in health innovation.

Future-Proof Skills

STEM literacy prepares students for high-impact careers in medicine, bioengineering, AI, and data science.

Africa Can Lead

We're not just preparing students to follow — we're preparing them to create African solutions for global problems.

Representation Matters

Children deserve to see people who look like them in labs, at conferences, and in textbooks — shaping the future of science.

"By planting seeds of knowledge early, Genes for Change ensures Kenya will not only consume global science — we’ll create it."
