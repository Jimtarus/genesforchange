Empowering Young Scientists in Kenya

Our research and resources initiative transforms classrooms into laboratories of innovation. With local context and global relevance, we foster the next generation of African geneticists.

Student Research Projects

We mentor high school students to conduct original research in genetics and molecular biology. Projects include studies on local plant genomics, disease genetics, and DNA barcoding.

Our students have been featured in national expos, showcasing community-centered genetic solutions.

Open Access Lab Protocols

Free, downloadable, and adapted to local conditions — our lab manuals enable affordable experiments in any Kenyan classroom. No fancy labs required.

Teacher Training Modules

From lesson plans to safety training and digital simulations, our resources support biology teachers across the country with up-to-date molecular biology knowledge.

Curated Learning Library

Interactive videos, podcasts, animations, and simplified readings. Designed for teenagers, translated in multiple local languages.

Updated monthly by Kenyan scientists.

Case Study: DNA Barcoding in Baringo

Partnering with a rural school in Baringo County, students used home-built DNA extraction kits to study plants around Lake Baringo. Their research was presented at the African Junior Science Forum and added to a national biodiversity database.

This success shows how accessible, locally-led science can put Kenyan youth at the forefront of global discovery.
